The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice Tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel whose company designed and built the Tower

The tower is 330 meters(1.083ft) tall, about the same height as an 81-storey building, and the tallest structure in Paris. Its base is square, measuring 125 meters(410 ft) on each side. During its construction, the Eiffel Tower surpassed the Washington Monument to become the tallest structure in Paris.
